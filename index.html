Rowan Atkinson

An English actor, comedian and writer

Here's a timeline of Rowan Atkinson

  • 1955 - born in Consett, County Durham, England
  • 1975 - he continued for the degree of MSc in Electrical Engineering at The Queen's College, Oxford, the same college where his father matriculated in 1935
  • 1976 - Won national attention in The Oxford Revue at the Edinburgh Festival Fring
  • 1978 - Started his acting career by appearing in a series of comedy shows for BBC Radio 3 called ‘The Atkinson People
  • 1979 - he starred in a television comedy sketch show titled, ‘Not the Nine O’ Clock News’ along with Pamela Stephenson, Mel Smith, Griff Rhys Jones and Chris Langham.
  • 1983 - 'The Black Adder' was written by Richard Curtis and Rowan Atkinson and also featured Atkinson. Voted as the ‘second-best British sitcom of all time’, this show was also ranked as the ‘20th best TV show of all time’. Rowan Atkinson, cast as Edmund Blackadder, was the series’ protagonist and established himself as one of the best actors of his time.
  • 1994 - Atkinson gained further recognition with the film, ‘Four Weddings and a Funeral’ and for his voiceover for the bird, Zazu, in Disney’s, ‘The Lion King’.
  • 1995 - His role as ‘Mr. Bean’ in the ‘Mr. Bean’ is considered one of his magnum opus’ because it is believed that he created this character when he was in college and he always wanted to portray such a character. As a personality who ‘rarely spoke’ on television, he enthralled audiences with his one-of-a-kind physical humor and his relations with various people and situations. During its successful five-year run on television, Atkinson became a national celebrity for his role and the series became so popular that it spawned two film adaptations
  • 2013 - He was honored with the title of ‘Commander of the Order of the British Empire
